I study representation and computation in sensory cortex, and use visual navigation in mice as a model.

To do this, I build custom rigs for mouse behavior in virtual reality and design novel tasks that require vision to solve. I make them complex, active, and naturalistic to challenge their perceptual robustness and to capture the interplay between action and sensory signals in visual cortex.

<!-- ![Figure 1](figure1.png) -->

Then, I use optical techniques for recording and manipulation from systems neuroscience to study how cortical processing works. I record the activity from thousands of cortical neurons at once, then use sophisticated modeling and machine learning to describe how information is formatted, where it is, how it's computed, and so on. 

<!-- ![Figure 1](figure1.png) -->


During my PhD in [Spencer Smith's lab](https://www.slslab.org), I described several distinctions in how higher visual areas represent vision and behavior, and also showed that behavior information in mouse visual cortex changes dramatically with changes in the visual input. This manuscript is in preparation to be submitted soon.
